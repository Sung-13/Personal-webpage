About Me

I am an Assistant Professor (Lecturer) in Sport Management at the University of Stirling, Scotland. I joined the university in August 2022, bringing an interdisciplinary academic background that spans law, tourism, and sport marketing and management.

I completed my PhD in Sport Marketing and Communication at Loughborough University in 2022. My doctoral research built upon earlier studies, including an MSc in Sport Management from Bournemouth University (2017), an MA in Tourism Science from Hanyang University (2015), and a BA in Law from Yonsei University (2012) in South Korea.

This diverse educational background enables me to approach research questions from multiple perspectives. I aim to bridge the gap between academic theory and practical applications that can enhance quality of life and inform evidence-based policy and practice.

In 2024, I obtained certification in Artificial Intelligence in Sport from the Academy for Sport Innovation and Technology Studies (AISTS) in Lausanne, Switzerland.

Research Interests

My research addresses contemporary challenges in public policy and sport consumer behaviour through two primary strands.

Policy Public Relations

Examining how effective communication strategies can enhance public engagement and increase policy support and compliance in the context of sport event management. This includes government-public relationships, crisis communication, and stakeholder engagement in mega sport events.

  • Government-public relationships
  • Mega sport event policy
  • Crisis communication
  • Public engagement strategies

Technology Adoption & Consumer Behaviour

Examining technologies such as augmented reality (AR), virtual reality (VR), smart wearable devices, connected fitness equipment, and AI-powered services. The primary goal is to determine how the use of these technologies influences wellbeing and how digital tools can support inclusion and reduce social inequality.

  • AR/VR in sport consumption
  • Esports and gaming
  • Smart wearable devices
  • AI in sport services

PhD Opportunities in the UK

I am actively seeking motivated PhD students to join me at the University of Stirling, Scotland, UK. If you are passionate about esports research, sport technology, or sport marketing, I would be delighted to discuss doctoral supervision opportunities with you.

PhD Research Areas

I welcome PhD proposals in the following areas:

  • Esports PhD: Fan behaviour, live streaming research, esports sponsorship, event marketing, market segmentation, and esports governance
  • Sport Technology PhD: AR/VR adoption in sport, smart wearables, AI-powered sport services, and their impact on consumer behaviour and wellbeing
  • Sport Marketing PhD: Fan engagement, online communities, brand equity, CSR in sport, and sport consumer behaviour
  • Policy Public Relations: Government-public relationships, crisis communication, stakeholder engagement in mega sport events like the Olympics

PhD Candidate Requirements

  • A strong academic background (typically a Master's degree in sport management, marketing, or related field)
  • Clear research interests aligned with esports, sport technology, or sport marketing
  • Strong quantitative or qualitative research skills
  • Excellent written and verbal communication skills in English
  • Self-motivation and ability to work independently
  • Passion for sport management research and academic career

How to Apply for PhD in UK

If you are interested in pursuing a PhD in the UK under my supervision, please send the following to my email:

  1. Your CV/Resume with academic background
  2. A brief research proposal (1-2 pages) outlining your PhD research interests
  3. Academic transcripts from your Master's degree
  4. A sample of your academic writing (if available)

Please use the subject line: "PhD Inquiry: [Your Name]"

PhD Funding in UK

Several funding options are available for PhD students in the UK:

  • University of Stirling PhD Scholarships
  • Scottish Funding Council Scholarships
  • Commonwealth Scholarships (for eligible countries)
  • Chevening Scholarships (for eligible countries)
  • Self-funded PhD options with flexible arrangements

I am happy to discuss funding strategies and support strong scholarship applications.
